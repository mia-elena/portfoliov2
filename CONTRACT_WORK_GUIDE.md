## 1. Pre-Launch: Foundation

### A. Positioning Strategy
**Choose Your Niche:**
- ‚úÖ Full-stack web applications (React + Node/Python)
- ‚úÖ API development & system integration
- ‚úÖ Educational technology platforms
- ‚úÖ Data dashboards & analytics tools
- ‚ùå Avoid: "I do everything" (dilutes expertise)

**Ideal Client Profile (ICP):**
- Industry: EdTech, SaaS startups, small agencies
- Budget: $5k-$25k per project
- Pain point: Need technical co-founder/senior dev without full-time commitment
- Decision maker: Technical founder, CTO, or product manager
- Company size: 5-50 employees (small enough to move fast, large enough to afford you)

**Your Value Proposition:**
```
"I help [EdTech companies] build [scalable web platforms]
that [reduce operational overhead by 40%] without [hiring
a full engineering team]."
```

### B. Rate Structure
**Hourly vs. Project-Based:**
- **Hourly ($75-$150/hr):** Good for ongoing maintenance, unclear scope
- **Project-Based ($5k-$50k):** Better for defined deliverables, higher perceived value
- **Retainer ($3k-$10k/month):** Best for long-term relationships

**Your Starting Rates (Based on Experience):**
- Discovery/Consulting: $100/hr
- Development Work: $85-$100/hr or project-based
- Maintenance/Support: $75/hr (lower because less cognitive load)

**Pricing Psychology:**
- Never charge <$50/hr (signals junior developer)
- Present 3 options: Good ($5k), Better ($10k), Best ($15k)
- Always anchor high, discount strategically

### C. Essential Business Setup
**Legal Entity:**
- [ ] Form LLC (asset protection, tax benefits) - $100-$500
- [ ] Get EIN from IRS (free, 5 minutes online)
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Get professional liability insurance ($500-$1k/year)

**Tools & Systems:**
- **Invoicing:** FreshBooks, Wave (free), or Stripe Invoicing
- **Contracts:** Bonsai, Docusign, HelloSign
- **Time Tracking:** Toggl, Harvest, Clockify
- **Project Management:** Linear, Notion, ClickUp
- **Communication:** Slack (free), Loom (video updates)
- **Proposals:** Proposify, PandaDoc, or Google Docs template

**Financial Management:**
- Separate checking account for business
- Set aside 25-30% for taxes (quarterly payments)
- Track ALL expenses (software, hardware, home office deduction)
- Use accounting software (QuickBooks Self-Employed, Wave)

### B. Pricing Strategy

**How to Calculate Your Rate:**
```
1. Determine your target annual income: $80,000
2. Divide by billable hours (assume 50%): 1,000 hrs/year
3. Hourly rate: $80,000 / 1,000 = $80/hr
4. Add 25% for overhead: $80 * 1.25 = $100/hr
5. Round to comfortable number: $100/hr
```

**Project-Based Pricing Formula:**
```
Estimated Hours * Hourly Rate * Buffer (1.2-1.5)

Example:
- 80 hours of work
- $100/hr rate
- 1.3 buffer (30% for unknowns)
= 80 * $100 * 1.3 = $10,400
```

**Presenting Options:**
- **Good:** Bare minimum, lowest price (30% below)
- **Better:** Recommended, best value (baseline)
- **Best:** Premium features, highest price (50% above)

**Psychology:**
- Most clients choose middle option
- High option makes middle look reasonable
- Low option sets floor (never go below)

```
1. **Reduce Scope:** We could start with [core features]

3. **Phased Payment:** Keep the scope, but break into 2

```

**"Can you do it for half?":**

```
- Using a no-code tool like Webflow/Bubble for MVP
- Revisiting when budget allows for full build

```

**"We'll give you equity":**

```
1. It's in addition to market-rate cash payment
2. I have significant input on product direction
3. There's a clear path to liquidity (funding, acquisition)

For most projects, I've found cash is cleaner for both sides.
```

- Payment schedule (NET 30 instead of NET 15)


   - Payment schedule (30/40/30 or 50/50)
   - Late payment fees (1.5% per month)
   - Accepted payment methods

3. **Timeline:**
   - Start and end dates
   - Key milestones
   - Client responsibilities (providing assets, feedback)

4. **Intellectual Property:**
   - Client owns final code upon full payment
   - You retain right to showcase in portfolio
   - Pre-existing code/libraries remain yours

5. **Revisions:**
   - Include X rounds of revisions per milestone
   - Additional revisions billed at hourly rate

6. **Termination:**
   - Either party can terminate with 7 days notice
   - Client pays for work completed to date
   - Partial refund of unearned deposit

7. **Confidentiality:**
   - Both parties agree not to share sensitive info
   - Survives contract termination

8. **Liability Limitation:**
   - Your liability capped at project value
   - Client responsible for content legality
   - No warranties beyond professional workmanship

9. **Dispute Resolution:**
   - Mediation before litigation
   - Governing law (your state)

### B. Protecting Yourself

**Red Flags in Client Contracts:**
- ‚ùå "All IP belongs to client, even pre-existing code" (too broad)
- ‚ùå "Unlimited revisions" (scope creep)
- ‚ùå "Payment upon project completion" (all risk on you)
- ‚ùå "Developer liable for all damages" (unlimited liability)
- ‚ùå Non-compete clauses (restricts future work)

**How to Push Back:**
```
"I appreciate the contract. A few items I'd like to adjust:

- Clause 4.2: I'd like to cap liability at the project value
  ($X,XXX) for both parties' protection.

- Clause 6.1: I retain rights to pre-existing code and
  open-source libraries. Client owns custom code created
  for this project.

- Clause 3.3: Let's define 'revisions' as 2 rounds per
  milestone, with additional rounds at $X/hr.

Can we revise these points? Happy to discuss on a call."
```

**When to Walk Away:**
- Client refuses reasonable contract changes
- Contract heavily favors one party
- "Just trust us, we don't need a contract" (biggest red flag)

### C. Protecting Yourself

**Prevention Tactics:**
1. **Kill Switch:** Host project on your account until paid
2. **Incremental Delivery:** Don't deliver final files until paid
3. **Contracts:** Always have signed agreement with payment terms
4. **Deposits:** Minimum 30% upfront (50% for new clients)

**When to Write Off:**
- Client disappears, won't respond
- Cost to collect > amount owed
- Bad for mental health (stress tax)

- Bad debts can be deducted as business loss
